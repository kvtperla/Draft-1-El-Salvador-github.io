Overall Analysis of all Advertisements

Across all cases, El Salvador’s image has shifted dramatically, from violence displayed in La Vida Loca, to sharing pride in Great Like Our People, to displaying adventurist locations like Surf City, and finally assuring security in the CECOT documentary. Through Hall’s theory of encoding/decoding model it highlights how audiences can negotiate the official narrative of each advertisement. Barthes’ also reveals how selective identities are naturalized, transforming the historical struggles into timeless symbols. Fraser’s critique of the public spheres, which are the advertisements themselves, display they silence counterpublics and build on creating a false reality of El Salvador. However, this is also built through the consumer themselves as Terranova’s framework emphasizes the role of unpaid digital engagement plays a role in amplifying this content. While Marx’s commodity fetishism also critiques the commodification of safety, pride, and identity.
